Info

A small private web app for a regular check-in with yourself. Runs entirely in your browser — no server, no account, no tracking.

Quick guide

💾
Save

Updates the most recent check-in for today. Use this to refine your entry during the day.

➕
Extra

Adds a new check-in alongside the existing one for today. Useful if your mood or energy shifts significantly.

⚙️
Settings

Choose your language, theme, and which check-in parts are visible. Hidden sections are not deleted — just not shown.

📋
Overview

Browse, search, sort and filter all your saved check-ins. Click a row to open it in the Check-in tab. Export or import entries as JSON.

What you can track

🌡️ Weather at check-in time
💭 Free-text thoughts
🎡 Core feeling via emotion wheel
🧍 Body signals on a body figure
⚡ Physical, mental & emotional energy levels
🎨 Mood via a 10×10 valence/arousal matrix
✅ Actions and intentions
📝 An optional free-text note

Privacy

✓ No server. Everything runs locally in your browser.
✓ No account or login required.
✓ No analytics, no cookies, no tracking.
✓ No personal data is collected or stored. No names, email addresses, or identifiers.
ℹ The optional weather widget contacts Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com). No personal data is sent — only your coordinates or city name.
ℹ Data is not encrypted by this app.

Storage

✓ Your data is stored in your browser localStorage on this device.
✓ The app saves entries and settings as plain JSON text in localStorage.
⚠️ Opening the app in a different browser or on a different device shows no data — localStorage is not shared between browsers or devices.
⚠️ Clearing your browser data or cache can permanently delete all your check-ins.
⚠️ In private/incognito mode, data is deleted as soon as you close the window.
💾 Back up your data

Use Export in the Overview tab to save a JSON backup to your computer. Use Import to restore it or move entries to another browser or device.

Version

How is the colour score calculated?

Each day in the history calendar gets a colour. The app looks at which check-in components you filled in, in this order:

  1. Emotion wheel If you picked an emotion (e.g. joy, fear, trust), that emotion's type determines the colour.
  2. Mood selector If no emotion wheel was used, the overall mood choice (Great / Okay / Low) is used.
  3. Mood grid (valence axis) If neither above was filled in, the horizontal valence axis of the circumplex grid is used: 7–10 = positive, 4–6 = mixed, 1–3 = low.

What do the colours mean?

Positive Emotion: joy, serenity, love, trust — Mood: Great — Valence: 7–10
Mixed Emotion: acceptance, surprise, anticipation — Mood: Okay — Valence: 4–6
Low Emotion: sadness, anger, fear, disgust, shame — Mood: Low — Valence: 1–3
No entry No check-in recorded for that day

Energy levels, body signals, and notes are not factored into the colour. They are visible when you click a day to load that entry.
